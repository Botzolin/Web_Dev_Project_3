The History of Arduino

Firstly, it would be wise to introduce the reader to the subject at hand. An Arduino is an open-source electronics platform consisting of a programmable circuit board (a microcontroller) and an Integrated Development Environment (IDE) for writing and uploading code. It was designed to be accessible to everyone interested in electronics.

The Origins

The story of Arduino began in 2005 at the Interaction Design Institute Ivrea in Italy. There, a small team managed to create a cheap microcontroller board.

The Name

The name Arduino comes from the Bar di Re Arduino, a small bar in Ivrea where the founders often met to discuss their ideas.

The bar itself was named after King Arduino of Ivrea, who ruled Italy in the 11th century.

The First Board

The first Arduino board was built around the ATmega8 microcontroller and used a programming environment related to Processing.

Global Impact

Arduino was adopted by millions of users worldwide and is used in education, simple automations and industrial automations.
